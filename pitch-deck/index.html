The problem
"The enemy after discharge isn't foreign. It's waking up with no formation, no unit, no mission."

1 in 3 veterans report significant social isolation post-service. The highest-risk window is the first two years after discharge — and no standard of care reaches them where they are.

Crisis hotlines help at the edge. They don't prevent the months of silence that leads there.

How isolation compounds

The cycle
that kills.

01  —  TRIGGER
Discharge
The unit dissolves. Years of structure, identity, and belonging disappear overnight. There is no offboarding from war.
02  —  COMPOUND
Silence
No squad. No formation. Civilian peers don't understand. Reaching out feels like admitting defeat.
03  —  CRISIS
Alone
Isolation deepens into depression. By the time crisis arrives, the veteran has already been unreachable for months.

The ecosystem

Same community.
Every screen.

The SUAS website at suasqrf.org is the home base — credibility, content, organization. The app is the field unit. They share one event calendar, one community feed, and one mission.

A veteran finds SUAS on Facebook or Google, lands on the site, and downloads the app. App users volunteer, donate, and share — driving traffic back. The loop closes. No veteran falls through the gap between screens.
